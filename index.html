What We Provide

  • A+

    Having the presence of A antigen, A positive blood type can accept A+ and A-, along with A and B antigen free blood types O+ and O-.

  • B+

    As in the case of 'A' or 'O'-type blood, an individual with a 'B' blood type may be Rh+ or Rh-, depending on whether there are Rh proteins on the red blood cells.

  • B-

    B- blood type is Rh negative. B Negative blood type has certain risk involved which happens during pregnancy when mother is Rh Negative and Fetus is Rh Positive.

  • AB-

    Having the presence of A and B antigens and the absence of the Rh antigen, AB negative blood type can only receive blood types from AB-, B-, A- and O-.

  • O+

    The O positive blood type is the most common and incredibly an important donor in maintaining adequate blood supply. It is present in 1 out of 3 individuals.

  • A-

    Having the presence of A antigen and absence of Rh antigen, A negative blood type can only accept A- and O-. Transfusions with any other blood group can trigger an immune response.

About Us

We Care For Every Life

Blood donation and transfusion service is an indispensable part of contemporary medicine and health care. Blood management has been recognized as a challenging task because of life threatening nature of blood products entails the punctilious administration due to its perishable nature & required timely processing and it also saves the life.

Eligibility

  • 1. Healthy volunteers of age group between 18-65 years of age, weight at least 45 kg and Hb of at least 12.5grams can donate blood.
  • 2. Healthy males can donate blood once in three months and women once in four months.
  • 3. A donor shall not do any type of donation within 12 months after a bone marrow harvest and within 6 months after a peripheral stem cell harvest.
  • 4. Pregnant women or recently delivered mother should be deferred for 12 months after delivery.
  • 5. People with HIV/AIDS, syphilis and hepatitis, and injected with drugs at any point of life, must never give blood.
